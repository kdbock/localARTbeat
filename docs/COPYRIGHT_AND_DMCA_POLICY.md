# ARTbeat Copyright And DMCA Policy

Effective date: March 26, 2026  
Last updated: March 26, 2026  
Owner: Local ARTbeat, LLC

ARTbeat respects copyright and expects users to upload only content they own or
have the legal right to use.

This document is an operational policy, not legal advice.

## Filing A Copyright Complaint

Send copyright complaints and takedown notices to:

- Email: `info@localartbeat.com`
- Mail: `Local ARTbeat, LLC, PO BOX 232, Kinston, NC 28502`

Include at minimum:

- your full name and contact information
- identification of the copyrighted work
- identification of the allegedly infringing content on ARTbeat
- a statement that you believe in good faith the use is not authorized
- a statement that the information provided is accurate
- a statement, under penalty of perjury where applicable, that you are the
  rights holder or authorized to act for them

## ARTbeat Response

When ARTbeat receives a sufficiently detailed complaint, ARTbeat may:

- remove or disable access to the content
- notify the affected user
- request more information
- preserve records relating to the complaint
- restrict or terminate repeat infringers

ARTbeat may act before receiving a court order when it believes removal is
appropriate to reduce risk or comply with platform obligations.

## Counter-Notice

If your content was removed for copyright reasons and you believe the removal
was incorrect, send a counter-notice to `info@localartbeat.com`.

Include:

- your name and contact information
- identification of the removed content
- a statement explaining why you believe the content was removed in error
- a statement that you consent to the applicable legal process where required

ARTbeat may forward a valid counter-notice to the original complainant where
appropriate.

## Repeat Infringer Policy

ARTbeat may suspend or terminate accounts that repeatedly upload infringing
content or repeatedly trigger credible copyright complaints.

## False Or Abusive Notices

Knowingly false, abusive, or fraudulent notices or counter-notices may result
in account action or legal escalation.
